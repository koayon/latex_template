# Rebuttals

After your paper is submitted to a conference, you will often get feedback on your paper in peer review.
You then have some time to take on this feedback to improve the paper.
The way that I'd recommend running this process is having the first author copy
all of the reviews into tabs in a Google Doc and colouring each part of the review to indicate
what kind of changes are required following the advice [here](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1cdEypaZXnJ10IckV49iBXEl27gCFnwEhQfLr680Fv18/edit?tab=t.0#heading=h.mt80mhtvp84q).
The first author should also add detailed comments to each of the criticisms in the Google Doc,
especially with lots of detail on how you would run additional experiments or add extra proofs/sections
if that's required.
Then you should distribute the work of the rebuttal between the authors - typically the first author(s)
will do most of the work here and minor authors may not do anything but exactly how you
allocate tasks depends on the situation with your paper.


Before starting your rebuttal you should read:

- [How to ML Rebuttal](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1cdEypaZXnJ10IckV49iBXEl27gCFnwEhQfLr680Fv18/edit?tab=t.0#heading=h.mt80mhtvp84q)
- [Neel's Guide to Rebuttals](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/vJNQZqgnKSxTBdFbS/neel-nanda-s-shortform?commentId=qqHPrBheFwQgrJbzN)

Both of these posts give a lot of detail on how to think about the rebuttals period.
Talking to your senior author is also a good idea as they'll have experience with rebuttals.

One vital thing to ensure is that the first author(s) are very available during the rebuttals period.
There can be a lot of work to do and you don't want to start late and reduce your chances of your
paper being accepted here.

Another important thing to note is that you can get ahead of the rebuttals.
If as you were finishing writing the paper, there's one part that you know you're going to be asked
about, prepare your response early so that you know you can nail it.
This typically includes running additional experiments or a new ablation so that you've covered your
bases.
